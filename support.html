Frequently Asked Questions

How does PingPulse diagnose network issues?
PingPulse sends lightweight TCP probes (not ICMP ping) to public DNS servers like Cloudflare (1.1.1.1) and Google (8.8.8.8) on port 443, and measures DNS resolution speed. These measurements are analyzed on-device to classify the root cause — Wi-Fi, router, ISP, DNS, or offline.
What happens after the 7-day free trial?
PingPulse continues working with basic monitoring at a 60-second refresh interval. Premium features — advanced analytics, reliability trends, incident grouping, export tools, and adaptive refresh controls — require a one-time purchase of $4.99. No subscription, no recurring charges.
What features are included in the free version?
After the trial, you still get: real-time monitoring (at 60s intervals), root cause diagnosis, timeline of events, recent log window, basic alerts, and the live heartbeat menu bar icon. Premium adds: adaptive refresh, advanced analytics, reliability score, trends, incident grouping, CSV/HTML export, and advanced alert controls.
Does PingPulse collect any personal data?
No. All diagnostics run entirely on your device. No data is collected, transmitted, or stored on external servers. See our Privacy Policy for full details.
Can I get a refund?
Refund requests are handled by Apple. Visit reportaproblem.apple.com to submit a refund request.
What can I export?
Three formats: diagnostics CSV (raw metrics), incident report CSV (grouped events), and detailed HTML health reports with hourly/daily/weekly range selectors. Export is a premium feature available during the trial and after purchase.
Why does PingPulse need network access?
Network access is required to perform the diagnostic probes that measure your connection quality. PingPulse connects only to Cloudflare (1.1.1.1) and Google (8.8.8.8) on port 443, and performs standard DNS lookups. No personal information is transmitted.
What macOS versions are supported?
PingPulse supports macOS 14.6 (Sonoma) and later. For the best experience with the Liquid Glass design, macOS 26 (Tahoe) is recommended.
The app shows "Unknown" — what does that mean?
When PingPulse first launches, it needs a few seconds to gather enough data to make a diagnosis. Keep the app running and it will classify your connection status within one or two probe cycles.

Troubleshooting

PingPulse shows "Offline" but I have internet: This can happen if your firewall or VPN is blocking TCP connections on port 443 to 1.1.1.1 or 8.8.8.8. Try temporarily disabling your firewall or VPN. You can also switch the probe target in Settings (premium feature).

Notifications aren't working: Make sure PingPulse has notification permissions enabled. Go to System Settings → Notifications → PingPulse and ensure "Allow Notifications" is turned on. Also check that quiet hours aren't active in the app settings.

The menu bar icon isn't appearing: If you have many menu bar items, macOS may hide some. Try closing other menu bar apps or using a tool like Bartender to manage menu bar space.

High CPU usage: This shouldn't happen under normal conditions. If it does, try increasing the refresh interval in Settings. The 5 MB rolling log buffer is designed to prevent memory bloat.

False alerts after waking from sleep: PingPulse includes sleep/wake-aware outage suppression. If you're still seeing false alerts, make sure you're running the latest version of the app.

Trial expired but I want to try again: The 7-day trial is a one-time offer tied to your device. If you'd like full access, you can purchase PingPulse for $4.99 — a one-time payment with no subscription.
