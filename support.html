Frequently Asked Questions

How does PingPulse diagnose network issues?
PingPulse sends lightweight TCP probes (not ICMP ping) to public DNS servers like Cloudflare (1.1.1.1) and Google (8.8.8.8) on port 443. It also measures DNS resolution speed. These measurements are analyzed on-device to classify the root cause of any connectivity problems.
Does PingPulse use a lot of bandwidth or battery?
No. Each probe is a minimal TCP connection (just a handshake, no data payload). PingPulse also features adaptive refresh that automatically reduces probe frequency when your connection is stable, minimizing resource usage.
Does PingPulse collect any personal data?
No. All diagnostics run entirely on your device. No data is collected, transmitted, or stored on external servers. See our Privacy Policy for full details.
Can I get a refund?
Refund requests are handled by Apple. Visit reportaproblem.apple.com to submit a refund request for your purchase.
Why does PingPulse need network access?
Network access is required to perform the diagnostic probes that measure your connection quality. PingPulse connects only to Cloudflare (1.1.1.1) and Google (8.8.8.8) on port 443, and performs standard DNS lookups. No personal information is transmitted.
Can I export my diagnostics data?
Yes. Go to the Settings tab and click "Export Log" to save a CSV file of your diagnostic history. This is useful for sharing with your ISP to demonstrate outage patterns.
What macOS versions are supported?
PingPulse supports macOS 13 (Ventura) and later. For the best experience with the new Liquid Glass design, macOS 26 (Tahoe) or later is recommended.
The app shows "Unknown" — what does that mean?
When PingPulse first launches, it needs a few seconds to gather enough data to make a diagnosis. Keep the app running and it will classify your connection status within one or two probe cycles.

Troubleshooting

PingPulse shows "Offline" but I have internet: This can happen if your firewall or VPN is blocking TCP connections on port 443 to 1.1.1.1 or 8.8.8.8. Try temporarily disabling your firewall or VPN to verify. You can also switch the probe target in Settings.

Notifications aren't working: Make sure PingPulse has notification permissions enabled. Go to System Settings → Notifications → PingPulse and ensure "Allow Notifications" is turned on. Also check that quiet hours aren't active in the app settings.

The menu bar icon isn't appearing: If you have many menu bar items, macOS may hide some. Try closing other menu bar apps or using a tool like Bartender to manage menu bar space.

High CPU usage: This shouldn't happen under normal conditions. If it does, try increasing the refresh interval in Settings (e.g., to 15–30 seconds) and enable "Reduce traffic automatically" to let the app throttle probes when your connection is stable.
