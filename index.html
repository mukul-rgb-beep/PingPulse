FAQ

Got questions?

How does PingPulse diagnose network issues?+
PingPulse sends lightweight TCP probes (not ICMP ping) to public DNS servers like Cloudflare (1.1.1.1) and Google (8.8.8.8) on port 443, and measures DNS resolution speed. These measurements are analyzed entirely on-device to classify the root cause of any connectivity problems.

Does PingPulse collect any personal data?+
No. All diagnostics run entirely on your device. No data is collected, transmitted, or stored on external servers. See our Privacy Policy for full details.
What macOS versions are supported?+
PingPulse supports macOS 14.6 (Sonoma) and later. For the best Liquid Glass experience, macOS 26 (Tahoe) or later is recommended.

What can I export?+
Three formats: diagnostics CSV (raw metrics), incident report CSV (grouped events), and detailed HTML health reports with hourly/daily/weekly ranges. Perfect for sharing with your ISP to prove outage patterns.
Does it drain battery or bandwidth?+
No. Each probe is a minimal TCP handshake with no data payload. Adaptive refresh reduces frequency when stable. A 5 MB rolling log buffer prevents memory bloat. Sleep/wake awareness avoids unnecessary probes.
