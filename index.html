FAQ

Got questions?

How does PingPulse diagnose network issues?+
PingPulse sends lightweight TCP probes (not ICMP ping) to public DNS servers like Cloudflare (1.1.1.1) and Google (8.8.8.8) on port 443. It also measures DNS resolution speed. These measurements are analyzed on-device to classify the root cause of any connectivity problems.
Does it use a lot of bandwidth or battery?+
No. Each probe is a minimal TCP connection â€” just a handshake, no data payload. PingPulse features adaptive refresh that automatically reduces probe frequency when your connection is stable.
Does PingPulse collect any personal data?+
No. All diagnostics run entirely on your device. No data is collected, transmitted, or stored on external servers. See our Privacy Policy for full details.
What macOS versions are supported?+
PingPulse supports macOS 13 (Ventura) and later. For the best Liquid Glass experience, macOS 26 (Tahoe) or later is recommended.
Is this a subscription or a one-time purchase?+
PingPulse is a one-time purchase of $6.99. No subscriptions, no recurring charges. All current features and future updates are included.
Can I export my diagnostics data?+
Yes! Go to the Settings tab and click "Export Log" to save a CSV file. Perfect for sharing with your ISP to demonstrate outage patterns.
